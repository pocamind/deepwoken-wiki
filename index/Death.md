# Death

Death is an important aspect of Deepwoken. You have 3 lives in Deepwoken, 2 of those lives are allowed above the sea and your final life is in [The Depths](The_Depths.md). When you're on your second life, your healthbar will be changed, showing you that you only have 1 life left before going to [The Depths](The_Depths.md). If you are on your second life and your power level increases, Your healthbar will revert and your lives will be restored. Dying on your second life sends you to [The Depths](The_Depths.md). When you die, you lose some of your notes and items, this loss can be decreased through [talents](Talents.md).

[The Depths](The_Depths.md) is essentially the underworld where you have to redeem yourself and prove your worth. Your goal in [The Depths](The_Depths.md) is to enter the Collesuem and stealth past enemy entities called Watchers. If spotted by Watchers you will have to fend off Assassination Angels. Once you stealth past the Watchers and make it inside the Collesuem you will reach an elevator that takes you back up to the Overworld.

Leaving Depths Solo

-   Level 1-10 Allowed to leave
-   Level 10-20 Unknown(Someone test this please)
-   Level 20-30 Angel
-   Level 30-40 Sharko
-   Level 40-50 Enhanced Sharko
-   Level 50-60 Enforcer

Leaving Depths Group

-   Sharko Fight
-   If everyone is high enough level, battle royale

You may have to fight an enemy to prove your worth at the top of the Collesuem. If you win and prove your worth you will have all your lives restored and be able to progress as normal. Depths is essentially The Gulag.

Speaking of death, it is very unwise to kill people at a low level as guards in Viglis and Etris will hunt you down.
