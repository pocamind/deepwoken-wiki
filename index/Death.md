# Death

_If you want to know more about where you go after dying twice, see [The Depths](The_Depths.md)._

## Description

Death is a core aspect of Deepwoken. Provided you have taken neither [Hollow nor Vow of Thorns](Echoes.md#upgrades-and-modifiers), you have three lives in Deepwoken. The first two lives are in the The Overworld, and the third is in [The Depths](The_Depths.md). However, if you are either in a [Voidzone](Voidzones.md) or the [Voidsea](Voidsea.md), you will functionally only have two lives as dying will immediately drown you and send you to The Depths.

When you die, you drop roughly half your notes and unequipped items, but the losses can be decreased via the talent [Robber Baron](Talents.md#escape-artist). Soulbound items are not dropped on death.

When you're on your second life, your health bar will be stained. Leveling up will revert the health bar back to normal, essentially giving your third life back. Dying with a stained health bar drowns you and sends you to Scyphozia, the first layer of The Depths.

The Depths is essentially the underworld. While there is much to do here, your time may be limited as the First Layer passively drives you [insane](Insanity.md). If you seek to escape, you may either consume a wish with the [Shrine of Yun'Shul](<Shrine_of_Yun'Shul,_Keeper_of_Hearts.md>) (provided you have a [Resonance](Resonance.md)), use an [Idol of Yun'Shul](Relics.md#utility), or reach the elevator within the Lost City of Celtor and face your sentence by the [Voices of the Depths](Voices_of_the_Depths.md) to [The Trial](The_Depths.md#the-trial). Most will end up facing their trial, to which there are two main ways to reach the elevator:

-   Go to the District of Commerce on the right side of the city, and ring two bells to open the gate leading to the center of the city.
-   Enter the City of the Drowned on the left side, and either pay 25 notes to operate the small elevator leading up to the center, or climb up the elevator shaft. Be warned that while this path is sometimes faster, there will sometimes be a [Nautilodaunt](Nautilodaunt.md) blocking your path.

Should you successfully escape, your lives will be restored, and you will respawn at your last set spawn point in The Overworld.

Should you fail to escape and perish, you will be sent to the [Fragments of Self](Fragments_of_Self.md). This is basically the afterlife, the final memories of the character. There is not much you can do here. You'll either have to wipe your character, or pay Robux (1,000) to buy the Drowned Favour in order to restore your character, which will respawn you in the Etrean Luminant. This is commonly referred to as using Last Wind (You can only use this once per character). Within the Fragments of Self is also the [Hippocampal Pool](Hippocampal_Pool.md), which you can use to pass down anywhere from two to six items (depending on your [Passdown Capacity](Echoes.md#upgrades-and-modifiers)) to your future character on the same slot.

_If you want to know more about the ways you can die, read the [Death Compendium](Death_Compendium.md)._

## Tips on not dying

-   Heal at every good opportunity, and keep an eye on your blood, hunger, and thirst bars. Ensure you have at least some food on you for your adventures, along with wood and a flint for a [campfire](Campfire_Pit.md).
-   It is unwise to kill people outside of lawless areas in towns as [the guards of said town will hunt you down](Reputation.md), and you will not survive unless you are powerful enough.
-   There are two types of in danger tagging: General danger (blue skull), and player combat (red skull). Player combat always takes priority over general danger (NPCs, environmental hazards, etc.). If you are in combat, you cannot menu by clicking the menu button in the top left corner. You can, however, log by quitting the game.
    -   If you are in a bad spot while in general danger, so long as you are above 60% of your maximum HP, you can safely log without losing a life.
    -   If you log while in player combat, you **will** lose a life. Therefore, it is recommended to always menu instead of quitting in general, as if you are in the middle of quitting the game, there is a possibility of someone putting you in player combat and costing you a life.
-   To avoid being [voidwalked](Bounty_Hunting.md), increase your [Reputation](Reputation.md) with Etrea [Etreaiconfaction](Etrea.md), The Central Authority [Authorityiconfaction](The_Central_Authority.md), The Hive [Hiveiconfaction](The_Hive.md), the Summer Company [Summericonfaction](The_Summer_Company.md), the Ignition Union, The Divers [Diversiconfaction](The_Divers.md), and The Knives of Eylis [Knives of Eylis](The_Knives_of_Eylis.md)
    -   If a player you don't recognize is coming towards you, and they look scary, menu. Especially if they have a black aura around them.
    -   If a player is clearly about to attack you, and you can't take them in a fight, run and menu.
    -   If you are in player combat, and you clearly aren't strong enough to fight back, run. Get good at dodging and running until you are out of player combat, then menu.
-   You are not safe in a guild. All placed guild locations are visible on the map. Take care of your business in your guild quickly, then leave the vicinity.
-   [Lower Erisia](Lower_Erisia.md) will sometimes have "freshie stompers" lurking. Always watch your back, especially when you're a low power.
-   Know your own capabilities. There is no shame in running and getting help if you are, or are about to be, in a bad spot.

## Notes

-   Entering a [Whirlpool](Whirlpools.md) sends you to the First Layer regardless of current lives, but it will NOT count as a drowning. Regardless, as you are in the Depths, you functionally still only have one life.
-   Dying to hunger and then immediately dying to hunger again in The Depths is restorable.
-   Falling into the void of the [First Layer](First_Layer.md) while above the notated threshold of your healthbar will not kill you. Instead, you will be sent to the [Second Layer](Second_Layer.md).

## Trivia

-   In [Lore](Lore.md), the first time you die, you become "injured" - not actually "dead" as in your life being taken away. The second time you die, you become "drowned" as you descend to the different layers of [The Depths](The_Depths.md). It is only when you die in The Depths will your character truly "die," as your soul is sucked downwards through the layers so fast, the mind is unable to process the information it receives and the soul is torn apart.
    -   Before [The Tides](Lore.md#the-tides), souls who perished ascended into the Moonseye above.
