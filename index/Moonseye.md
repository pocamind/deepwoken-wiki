# Moonseye

_This page is about the celestial object and its symbolism. For other uses, see [Moon (disambiguation)](<Moon_(disambiguation).md>)._

“ AH! ALL SO CLEAR! THE MOONSEYE! NOT A REFLECTION, NO - ITS OWN LIGHT! NO WONDER IT IS SO COVETED! ”

― Soothsayer about the Moonseye

  

## Description

The Moonseye is one of the two celestial objects between the Surface and the Heavens - standing as Deepwoken's equivalent of the Moon and the counterpart to the [Suncross](Suncross.md). Unlike the Suncross, it is suspended between the surface and the Heavens at an equal distance.

It is a recurring symbol across Deepwoken, being referenced in items, locations, and aspect descriptions.

The Moonseye is a small hollowed circle with two vertical protrusions and has a smaller white circle within, hence giving it the "eye". A swirling vortex and a faint ray of light surrounds it.

## Cultural aspects

The people of Lumen have taken to unique perceptions about the Moonseye. Generally, it is viewed as a representation of life, death, and rebirth.[\[1\]](#cite-note-1)

### Religion

In Capran tradition, Jurik is often associated with the Moonseye, viewing it as an observer and an embodiment of the material forces of the world.

[The Moonlit Faith](The_Moonlit_Faith.md) is a religion dedicated to the worship of the Moonseye, believing that it is the gateway to the afterlife. In turn, sacrifice is needed for an entry into the afterlife.

The Starkindred Faith see the Moonseye as a gate to a promised land that they will journey into following the banishment of the Deep and the revival of Celestials. They also believe that the souls of deceased Celestials make their way into the Moonseye for a promised day.

## Notes

-   The Moonseye projects a Light that is believed to be the same as a Lightkeeper's.
-   The Celestials, namely [Zhatarn](Zhatarn.md) had siphoned souls out of the Moonseye for power, causing said souls pain as they are ripped away from their eternal rest.[\[2\]](#cite-note-2)
-   A wide amount of evidence supports the idea that the Moonseye contains a mass of souls, and if proven true, may hold the souls of the [Old Dead](Glossary.md#the-old-dead).
    -   Following the Tides, the Moonseye no longer pulls souls of the departed into it, but rather the Depths.
        -   The Depths has a stronger pull on souls than the Moonseye. Even so, the Moonseye's pull is still noticeable, given the swirling vortex that surrounds it.
    -   The mural when reading the [Moonseye Tome](Moonseye_Tome.md) depicts souls floating towards the Moonseye.
-   The Moonseye also has an abundant relation to [insanity](Insanity.md). This can be seen in the Moonseye Tome, which is a [prophet-turned-insane](Prophet_Pariah.md)'s dream, and the Blessing of the Moonseye Talent in [Vow of Iron](Vow_of_Iron.md).
