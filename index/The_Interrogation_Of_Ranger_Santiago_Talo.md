The Interrogation Of Ranger Santiago Talo

> (SUNSDAWN, 9TH BLOOMFALL, 904 CE)
> 
> // TRANSCRIPT OF MEETING WITH RANGER SANTIAGO TALO - CONDUCTED BY: RANGER COMMANDER ULYSSES DISII, BLACK DIVER - TRANSCRIBED BY: SCRIBE MEMNOS TULAD
> 
> \--
> 
> COMMANDER ULYSSES DISII: Now that the scribe is writing down our conversation, Ranger Talo, let me repeat the crux of our discussion hereto once again, for the written record. You diverted your scouting party away from your assigned patrol path, heading towards the direction of the Landfall. And you did this despite your superiors' direct orders to the contrary, which command all scouts to give the area a wide berth. Did you order this course of action?
> 
> RANGER SANTIAGO TALO: Commander, with all due respect, I must state again--
> 
> CMDR: Answer the question, ranger.
> 
> RNGR: Yes.
> 
> CMDR: Were you given permission to divert the group from the assigned route by your superiors?
> 
> RNGR: No, Commander. \[subject hesitated\]  
> \--  
> CMDR: Why did you defy direct orders from your superiors?
> 
> RNGR: Things had settled in the area, and it was quiet. We heard a strange commotion, and considered that there might be a threat to the other active Diver groups in the area. Therefore, we decided to investigate.
> 
> CMDR: And did it ever occur to you that this was a reckless course of action, Ranger? Your party was assigned a substantial detachment of fresh Diver recruits, and your patrol mission was meant to provide training and onboarding for them. If High Command has not issued a scouting mission to the vicinity of the ruins yet, they must have good reason to believe the area remains dangerous - and you led a squadron of rookies there, for Akarad's sake! What were you thinking?  
> \--  
> RNGR: We figured it might have been someone in trouble, Commander. It sounded like voices. I assessed that we could try aiding them.
> 
> CMDR: Yes, and surely your training taught you of the effect the Depths have on the mind, Ranger? Or have you forgotten of the audiovisual hallucinations and delusions Divers experience from long-term exposure? Are you telling me that you advanced - again, in defiance of direct orders! - without even being sure of what it was you were investigating, or if it was even real?
> 
> RNGR: The others heard the voices clearly, too, so I ruled out the possibility of hallucinations.
> 
> CMDR: And so you advanced?
> 
> RNGR: I followed the protocol--  
> \--  
> CMDR: Up to that point. That's irrelevant, given your insubordination. How did you proceed?
> 
> RNGR: I went forward myself, first. I led a small detachment of the more seasoned members of the party at the vanguard.
> 
> CMDR: Whom did you assign to this task alongside yourself?
> 
> RNGR: Ranger Emirad and Ranger Bykof.
> 
> CMDR: And you left the new recruits in the rear, unattended by seniority? Did you suffer a mind-scrambling head injury during your last mission, Ranger? Is your brain leaking out of your ears? Were I not bound by regulations, I ought to --
> 
> //\[REDACTED\]// \[commander reasonably displeased\]
> 
> Scribe Tulad, please strike that last statement from the record.  
> \--  
> Your admission of insubordination is damning, Ranger Talo, and disciplinary action will have to be taken. Unfortunately for me - and maybe fortunately for you, - your punishment will not be decided by me. Your suggestion of intel complicates matters, and I will need to file a report on the incident, which is why Scribe Tulad here is transcribing this meeting for reference.
> 
> RNGR: I understand, Commander.
> 
> CMDR: What happened next? Your empty-headed self led the vanguard?
> 
> RNGR: Yes, the van advanced. We encountered the bodies of two recently deceased individuals.
> 
> CMDR: How did you determine the deaths were recent?  
> \--  
> RNGR: I judged by the voices we heard. It sounded like someone calling for help. It also looked like there had been a recent struggle. Marks on the ground and such.
> 
> CMDR: A struggle? Against?
> 
> RNGR: A stray thresher, Commander. It was devouring the bodies. As we know, threshers do not scavenge, and seek mostly fresh prey. Myself, Ranger Emirad, and Ranger Bykof dispatched of the beast without issue.
> 
> CMDR: The splint on your arm suggests otherwise, Ranger.
> 
> RNGR: The thresher clamped down onto my arm, but Ranger Emirad dealt the killing blow to the beast moments after. The others were uninjured. The surgeons told me that the crushing force broke several bones, but I was still capable of completing our assignment.  
> \--  
> CMDR: How resolute of you. Should we clap? Should the High Convocation throw you a party? Should we invite Drallis Ehr? You're lucky you did not lose your arm, you --
> 
> //\[REDACTED\]// \[commander reasonably displeased\]
> 
> CMDR: Scribe Tulad, strike the last segment from the record once again, please. This should go without saying. We are in understanding? Good. As for you, Ranger, I hope you understand how incredibly lucky you were during this moronic excursion of yours, given that our scouts are not equipped for prolonged direct confrontation. Now, you mentioned bodies - who were the casualties?  
> \--  
> RNGR: Surface folk, Commander. Celtorian citizens. By my reckoning, survivors of the disaster. Until the thresher got to them, anyway. They looked ragged, and had signs of heavy injury.
> 
> CMDR: Our initial intel indicated there were no survivors.
> 
> RNGR: I am aware, Commander. But these people were decidedly from Celtor. Ranger Bykof can attest to it, being from the city himself. He recognised the distinct style of clothing. He said they looked upper class.
> 
> CMDR: Scribe Tulad, make a note for a meeting with Ranger Bykof in my schedule. High Command will want to hear more of this. Was there anything else, Ranger?
> 
> RNGR: We advanced the rest of our unit to rejoin the vanguard, and... We saw it, Commander. The city. Or what's left of it.  
> \--  
> CMDR: Of course you have. Every bloody living thing in the Depths has seen it. Hard to miss those hulking ruins given their size. What else?
> 
> RNGR: We didn't just see it from afar, Commander. We ended up quite close to it.
> 
> CMDR: And? Was there anything of interest to be seen, or are you wasting our time here?
> 
> RNGR: We saw that the city was fractured. It looked like it was sundered along several fault lines, and the elevation was all broken up. The outer walls and districts were in shambles, but we saw that the centre of the city looked mostly intact.
> 
> CMDR: Intact?  
> \--  
> RNGR: Yes. The cathedral in the city centre was still standing. We could see the walls and the cathedral spire from our location. Some of the structures had been damaged, though. We also saw movement at the gates, but we couldn't make out what the situation was. Looked like some small group of people heading into the city, advancing slowly. If I may offer a comparison, their pace was not unlike a funeral procession.
> 
> CMDR: What did they look like?
> 
> RNGR: We couldn't see close enough, Commander. Dark clothing, perhaps armour, but certainly not Diver attire. Ranger Bykof suggested some of them looked like the Celtorian city militia, but given the distance, we could not confirm this.
> 
> CMDR: Interesting. What next?
> 
> RNGR: We decided not to journey further, and returned to our assigned route.  
> \--  
> CMDR: It sounds like your brain found a way to plug the leak in your ears at the last moment. Ranger, this entire incident is a gross breach of scouting protocols. Your actions also potentially endangered inexperienced recruits. Do you understand the danger you placed your unit in?
> 
> RNGR: Yes, Commander.
> 
> CMDR: Do you understand you were needlessly putting yourself and your comrades at risk of death?
> 
> RNGR: Yes, Commander.
> 
> CMDR: And do the few drops of intellect still rattling about in your hollow skull understand that this misstep could cost you your position in the Divers organisation?
> 
> RNGR: Yes, Commander. But you must understand--  
> \--  
> CMDR: I will not hear it. Your orders were clear. You've already admitted your insubordination. Nevertheless...
> 
> Up until this point, our intel on the Landfall was incredibly limited. Even though your reckless actions were idiotic, the information gleaned by your unit will likely be of interest to High Command. Although your punishment will not be in my power, I will strongly recommend your assignment to sanitation duties at the Citadel for two weeks. However, as a former Ranger myself, it occurs to me you may be suffering from maladies of the mind. Prolonged exposure to the environment of the Depths can impair a Diver's judgement and critical thinking abilities. Thus, in my report to High Command, I will also include a recommendation for a week's leave, for recuperation.
> 
> RNGR: I understand. Thank you, Commander.
> 
> CMDR: If that is all, you may be dismissed.  
> \--  
> RNGR: Commander, if I may ask a question before I go?
> 
> CMDR: Make it quick.
> 
> RNGR: If there are survivors in the city, shouldn't we be mounting rescue operations?
> 
> CMDR: This is beyond my power, Ranger. \[subject hesitated\]
> 
> RNGR: I see.
> 
> CMDR: Under current conditions, it would be too dangerous to risk the lives of our Divers. We lose too many each year as is, and our recruitment numbers are low at the moment. In my opinion, if we had a forward operating base in Scyphozia, perhaps we could. But as you already know, High Command is intensifying scouting missions in the region for that same reason. These recent... happenings... might start a fire under the seats of the Convocants, though. Maybe it's about time things got moving.  
> \--  
> RNGR: I understand, Commander. Thank you.
> 
> CMDR: Dismissed.
> 
> // END OF TRANSCRIPT
