# Megalodaunt

Template:Creature Infobox V3

  

## Description

Megalodaunts, commonly refered to as Sharkos (not to be confused with the shark-like creature, the [Lionfish](Lionfish.md)) or Carl, are Giant Type enemies which spawn in The Etrean Luminant and The Depths.

It is the most common Giant Type enemy. Typically, killing a Megalodaunt grants a chest with decent gear, usually better than the loot which bandit camps yield.

This monster is hostile to every other creature and will attack them on sight, with the exception of others of its kind. It will, however, still attack its rarer, red variant. Added to the normal (greyish white) and red, there is also a black version of the Megalodaunt.

Seen mostly in Lower Erisia and around the Erisia Island, The Megolodaunt is the first Monster new players would stumble against. Players that lack insight of this creature and are Ill-equipped are mostly likely going to experience a swift death. Upon being killed, it drops his Hide.(use five of these to unlock a boss fight against primadon, also can be sold for notes.

## Moves

-   **Slash:** A short range slash. Blockable and parriable. Heavy posture damage.
-   **Kick:** Sends the target flying. Breaks block, but can be dodged.
-   **Jump:** If the target is above it, the Megalodaunt can jump, which incurs a multi-hit attack in a wide radius in front of it. Blockable and parriable.
-   **Spit:** A multi-hit projectile targeted at a single target. Blockable and parriable.
-   **Roar:** Can roar at nearby targets, placing them in combat and inflicting a small amount of insanity.
-   **Thousand Needles:** A multi-hit AoE move, often used against groups of targets. Blockable and parriable.
-   **Curb Stomp** - Megalodaunt ends his opponent's life by delivering a stomp onto their bodies, creating a gruesome crunching noise. This can happen almost under a second.

## Combos

-   Slash + Slash: If you block the first hit instead of parrying, the second hit will guard-break.
-   Slash + Kick: You can block and parry the slash, but the kick must be dodged.

## Tips

-   Megalodaunt lets out a gnarling snarl when he spawns in, notifying the player of his presence.
-   It is recommended to get atleast Adept Armor and Power Level 15+, unless you're a Chad.
-   Common slang terms of Megalodaunt is Sharko, Hugo, Gary, Jerry, and Big guy.
-   If Megalodaunt has you as a Target, its best if you parry some of his moves than try to avoid it. Moves such as Thousand Needles and Yellow Burst is semi-impossible to avoid, as it covers a good amount of area.
-   Your camera shakes the closer Megalodaunt is to your character, due to his footsteps.
-   It's always recommended to perfect dodge the Football Kick.
-   After parrying it's Palm Slap, block for a second to see if it slaps again and react to it's next move.
-   Megolodaunts have 1000 points of HP.
-   Although they do look extremely intimidating they can be dealt with if you have over 160 health or high fortitude, or if you have 2 or more people you can juggle the Megalodaunt between each of you and when one of you fighting gets low you swap out and heal.
-   If your above level 20 and have a squad of more than 3 players you can usually get away with just rushing them

-   With decently high HP and a good armor, it is perfectly viable to tank all the attacks and still go for hits; however, it is still better to block and parry what you can.
-   Along with a few other Giant Type enemies, the Megalodaunt gets stunned for a few seconds upon receiving a certain amount of damage.
