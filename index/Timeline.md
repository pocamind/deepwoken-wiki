# Timeline

## Old World

This section of the timeline does not have a specific year range. Historians believe that it predates the "The Tides" and the concept of measuring time through years.

### Historical Context

Before the arrival of the Tides, most of the land was populated by a variety of animals, including domesticated, predatory, and livestock species. In addition, animals were not only used for their resources but also served as means of transportation.[\[1\]](#cite-note-1). Alongside this, a currency system[\[2\]](#cite-note-2) predating notes relied on coins, with quarters serving as a measurement unit.

There was once an above-ground citadel inhabited by the great nation of [Celtor](Lost_Celtor.md), nowadays known as The City of the Drowned. It was renowned for its prosperity and had a vibrant marketplace with active trade. Among the visible remnants is the Cathedral of the Interstice, the purpose of which during that time remains a mystery, and its existence at the time is uncertain. However, there is speculation that the Cathedral of the Interstice might have been one of the Interstitial Lighthouses, given the similarity between their names.[\[3\]](#cite-note-3)

Another trace from the Old World are the Old Texts, which comprise a collection of historical records inscribed in glyphs or preserved within books. However, their existence in the current world remains uncertain, concealed in mystery and the passage of time. It is believed that pieces of this collection are housed within The Condemned Archives, a forbidden repository which texts were once deemed too dangerous for common consumption. The exact location of the Archives remains unknown, and it appears intentional that we are not meant to discover its whereabouts. [\[4\]](#cite-note-4)

### Pre-Canticlysm Period

During this period, mankind made the discovery of the Song and gradually refined its utilization into a vague yet powerful force. Nonetheless, its application remained limited and infrequent. As a result, cultural practices and techniques revolving around the Song, as we now understand them, had not yet been created or extensively researched.
