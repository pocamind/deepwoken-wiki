## Description

This page presents a possible reconstruction of the timeline of events that occurred within the world of Deepwoken.

## Old World

This section of the timeline does not have a specific year range. Historians believe that it predates the "The Tides" and the concept of measuring time through years.

### Historical Context

Before the arrival of the Tides, most of the land was populated by a variety of animals, including domesticated, predatory, and livestock species. In addition, animals were not only used for their resources but also served as means of transportation.[\[1\]](#cite-note-1) In the present world, there are five major landmasses, but during the era of the Old World, the Luminants were predominantly interconnected, with the World's Edge delineating its outer limits. The World's Edge, now known as the Voidsea, proved to be impassable at all times.[\[2\]](#cite-note-2) Alongside this, a currency system[\[3\]](#cite-note-3) predating notes relied on coins, with quarters serving as a measurement unit.

Another trace from the Old World are the Old Texts[\[4\]](#cite-note-4), which comprise a collection of historical records inscribed in glyphs or preserved within books. However, their existence in the current world remains uncertain, concealed in mystery and the passage of time - keepers of knowledge known as Old Stewards may be in possession of such vestiges and further knowledge about the Old World. It is believed that some pieces of this collection are housed within The Condemned Archives, a forbidden repository which texts were once deemed too dangerous for common consumption. The exact location of the Archives remains unknown, and it appears intentional that we are not meant to discover its whereabouts.[\[5\]](#cite-note-5)

On a more enigmatic note, the origin of the Adret aspect is intriguing and sets them apart from other aspects in the world, as they do not share any animal characteristics like the Felinors with cats, Canors with dogs or Vesperians with beetles. This distinction is a primary point of contention for many aspects, who view the Adrets as unfamiliar and alien. It is important to note that the Adret aspect is not associated with fish either.[\[6\]](#cite-note-6) It is believed that historically they had a tendency to be isolationist, dwelling in the mountainous regions and rarely interacting with other aspects until the advent of the Tides. However, with their tallest mountains now submerged, the Adrets have emerged into the wider world.

### Pre-Canticlysm Period

During this period, mankind made the discovery of the Song and gradually refined its utilization into a vague yet powerful force. Nonetheless, its application remained limited and infrequent. As a result, cultural practices and techniques revolving around the Song, as we now understand them, had not yet been created or extensively researched.

## The Canticlysm

### Arrival of The Canticlysm

As the utilization of the Song grows and understanding of it deepens, it culminated and reached its peak during a distinct period referred to as The Canticlysm. This period, often referred to as the Golden Age of the Song, is renowned for its supposed peace and tranquility and characterized by remarkable innovation.

During this transformative era, significant advancements lead to the creation of the four primary attunements known as [Flamecharm](Flamecharm.md), [Frostdraw](Frostdraw.md), [Thundercall](Thundercall.md), [Galebreathe](Galebreathe.md), and the newly emerging attunement called [Ironsing](Ironsing.md). The formation and development of Ironsing began after the discovery of pluripotent alloys, marking a significant milestone in the understanding and manipulation of metallic objects.[\[7\]](#cite-note-7) Additionally, legendary weapons of rare and mysterious nature, such as the Crypt Blade and the Curved Blade of Winds, are said to have been forged, along with many others whose existence remains shrouded in mystery.

It is believed that, despite how it seems the four primary Attunements were created during the Canticlysm, that they, [Shadowcast](Shadowcast.md), and other Attunements possibly, were actually merely discovered, as they seem to come from the Drowned Gods and be inherent to the Song, however other Attunements were created by mankind, artificial to the Song, such as Lifeweave, [Bloodrend](Bloodrend.md) and Ironsing.

## The Tides

An event that happened near the end of The Canticlysm and continued a few years afterwards, it was called the first Great Drowning. Its cause was the Drowned Gods, who were almost woken up by the overuse of The Song. They exerted their power over Lumen, corrupting the Sea. This caused immense destruction, dividing the world into the 5 Luminants. Alongside this, they caused the Voidfog to be drawn to the Sea, merging them into the Voidsea. Raging on, it led to the formation of [the Ministry](The_Ministry.md). The tides ended with the Great Drowning of Celtor, in which the Celtorian Horror dragged The City of Celtor underwater and into the first layer of [The Depths](The_Depths.md) - [Scyphozia](First_Layer.md). It caused [The Moonseye](Moonseye.md), a symbol of the afterlife, to be replaced with the Depths; When you die, rather than ascending, you now [drown](Death.md), taken into the depths. Escape is only possible through the [Depths trial](First_Layer.md#escaping-first-layer) or by making [a wish](<Shrine_of_Yun'Shul,_Keeper_of_Hearts.md>), but doing so will pull you back in eventually due to the pull on your soul, but certain rituals can negate this effect.

## The Resurgence/The New World

The Resurgence is the period of time we're currently in. It is when mankind started to adapt to the calamities brought by the Tides. It's said to have started sometime in the fifth century BCE. It's referred to as a dying world by the game's description.
