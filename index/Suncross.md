# Suncross

“ THE WANDERING STAR! ITS MOVEMENTS SO CALCULATED! ITS WARMTH - ITS LIGHT! SUCH BEAUTY - SUCH INTENT! ”

― Soothsayer about the Suncross

  

## Description

The Suncross is one of the two stars that orbits between Lumen and the Heavens - standing as Deepwoken's equivalent of the sun. Orbiting alongside the [Moonseye](Moonseye.md), the Suncross is partly responsible for Deepwoken's day/night cycle. The Suncross is also responsible for the turn of Summer and Winter, as the Suncross periodically orbits closer to and farther away from Lumen. During periods wherein the Suncross is farther from Lumen and closer to the Heavens, Lumen experiences Winter - and the reverse is true for Summer, during which the Suncross is orbiting closer to Lumen and farther away from the Heavens.

The Suncross is always seen as a white star with six distinct points. The Suncross can only be seen during the daytime, and cannot be witnessed during nighttime, retreating into the Voidfog - during which the Moonseye replaces its position in the sky.

The Suncross is an infrequently-used symbol in Deepwoken - particularly when compared to its counterpart, the Moonseye. While the Suncross generally represents light, warmth and comfort - the star has been morphed to embody the [Celestials](Celestial.md). This is primarily due to the efforts of the [Starkindred](Oath%253A_Starkindred.md), who first associated the Suncross with the Celestials following their acquisition of a Celestial shaped like the Suncross.

The Suncross and Moonseye are comparable to the [Yin and Yang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yin_and_yang), with one representing the darkness and another representing the light. Despite their dueling motifs, the two stars have purpose unique to one-another - the Moonseye was formerly responsible for the afterlife, and the Suncross' purpose (or former purpose) currently stands as unknown.

## According to the [Glossary](Glossary.md)...

• The bringer of life and warmth to Lumen.

• At night, the Suncross retreats into the Voidfog and its light dims, darkness falling over Lumen. At midday, the Suncross is at its least obscured at the Voidfog.

• The Suncross follows a elliptical path over Lumen, with the centre of its orbit seemingly beyond the Voidfog.

• Its orbit is roughly equidistant between the Surface and the Heavens, though it oscillates on its lateral axis yearly, and as mentioned, it pitches towards the Surface in summer, and the Heavens in winter. The climate is typically colder and more changeble in Nothern Lumen, and a more consistently warm tempature in Southern Lumen.

• Also as mentioned, the Moonseye can be seen as the Suncross's counterpart.
