# Suncross

## Description

The Suncross is one of the two stars that orbits between Lumen and the Heavens - standing as Deepwoken's equivalent of the sun. Orbiting alongside the [Moonseye](Moonseye.md), the Suncross is partly responsible for Deepwoken's day/night cycle. The Suncross is also responsible for the turn of Summer and Winter, as the Suncross periodically orbits closer to and farther away from Lumen. During periods wherein the Suncross is farther from Lumen and closer to the Heavens, Lumen experiences Winter - and the reverse is true for Summer, during which the Suncross is orbiting closer to Lumen and farther away from the Heavens.

The Suncross is always seen as a white star with six distinct points. The Suncross can only be seen during the daytime, and cannot be witnessed during nighttime - during which the Moonseye replaces its position in the sky.

The Suncross is an infrequently-used symbol in Deepwoken - particularly when compared to its counterpart, the Moonseye. While the Suncross generally represents light, warmth and comfort - the star has been morphed to embody the [Celestials](Celestial.md). This is primarily due to the efforts of the [Starkindred](Oath%253A_Starkindred.md), who first associated the Suncross with the Celestials following their acquisition of a Celestial shaped like the Suncross. While the Suncross is not named outright, the [Soothsayer](Soothsayer.md)'s following statement refers to the Suncross specifically:

"THE WANDERING STAR! ITS MOVEMENTS SO CALCULATED! ITS WARMTH - ITS LIGHT! SUCH BEAUTY - SUCH INTENT!"

The Suncross and Moonseye are comparable to the [Yin and Yang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yin_and_yang), with one representing the darkness and another representing the light. Despite their dueling motifs, the two stars have purpose unique to one-another - the Moonseye was formerly responsible for the afterlife, and the Suncross' purpose (or former purpose) currently stands as unknown.

Some refer to the Suncross as Sol.[\[1\]](#cite-note-1)
