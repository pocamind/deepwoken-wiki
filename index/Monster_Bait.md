# Monster Bait

“ Winter 1291 marks that fateful night, when the rumbling of a million Megalodaunts thundered in our ears. They laid waste to our homes... And what little lands we had left to squabble over. ”

― "The Dangers of Overfishing," page 42

Monster Bait

## Overview

Monster Bait are craftable items in Deepwoken. It costs Chum to craft, and prior knowledge of the recipe from [Mortus](Mortus.md), housed in a shack a ways away from the alternative shore of [Lower Erisia](Lower_Erisia.md).

Like the name suggests, it is used to spawn monsters. The three craftable baits can be used to spawn [Megalodaunts](Megalodaunt.md), [Threshers](Thresher.md) and [Terrapods](Terrapod.md) respectively. Megalodaunt and Thresher Bait costs 3 [Chum](Chum.md), and Terrapod Bait costs 2 [Chum](Chum.md).

## How to Use

To use Monster Bait, it has to be placed near the sea (in both elevation and proximity to the water). Terrapod Monster Bait, however, also allows being placed near a "temperate woodland," which seems to refer to anywhere on [Erisia](Lower_Erisia.md). It must not be within a certain radius of another Monster Bait and has a 30 second cooldown before you can place it again. Then, you must wait a varying amount of time for it to spawn. Times can range from nearly half a minute to upwards of 5 minutes for the monster to spawn in the place of the bait.

Monsters spawned through bait _do not_ give chests.

## Trivia

-   On the 13th of March, 2022, Megalodaunt Bait was added into the game. However, due to improper testing, the item wouldn't be removed from the player's inventory upon use, and there was nothing to stop players placing large amounts of bait in the same place, causing comically large numbers of Megalodaunts to spawn at once and also causing most of the resulting Sharkos' animations to break, so servers were filled with many, especially in Etris.  
    It caused the developers to shut down servers and disable the item, and the contents of Chests were doubled for 3 days to commemorate this event. This is now considered lore as "Sharko Day," as said by the developers, trying to cover up the mistake.
    -   There were even entire gangs of high levels server-hopping and beelining to Megalodaunt-infested hotspots to try to protect freshspawns and kill the Megalodaunts.
    -   Many refer to the event as 'The Sharko Rumbling,' a reference to the rumbling from Attack On Titan, which happened to be airing around that time. The latest version log in the wiki reflects this disastrous event as **"The Dreaded Winter of 1291."**
    -   One of the shutdown warning notes read "Shutdown at XX:57, good night to sharkos across the world"
-   Monsters spawned through bait seem to give less EXP than their naturally-spawning counterparts.
-   Megalodaunt Bait is easily the most sought-after bait as they can rarely spawn their Crimson counterpart, which in and of itself can drop the incredibly sought-after [First Light](First_Light.md), although at a very low rate.
-   [Destroyman III](Destroyman_III.md) mentions that taxes are worse than the Sharko Rumbling, implying that the Internal Revenue Sharkos are ruthless when it comes to tax collection.
-   Though Monster Bait was heavily nerfed, it is still possible to spawn essentially infinite mobs as long as you have the [Chum](Chum.md) and the patience to do so. This is especially true with Terrapods at Erisia, since no shore is necessary, as most of Lower Erisia counts as "temperate woodlands."
    -   Furthermore, due to the cheaper cost of Terrapod Bait and an infinite source of Chum from Mortus's shack nearby, if you have the patience and want to spam mobs everywhere, you can flood Lower Erisia with ease.

## Bugs

-   Sometimes upon use of bait, the item will be removed but the bait won't be placed.
    -   You can avoid this by always placing beneath your character in first person mode
-   Occasionally, after a mob spawns on bait, the bait will remain in place, though this is only visual (this does seem to affect the ability to place more bait near it, however) and no extra mobs will spawn. It will despawn on its own eventually.
