## Description

The Elder Edenkite, often shortened to just Edenkite,[\[3\]](#cite-note-skyreap-3) were said to be an immortal species of flying wyrms from the [Northern Luminant](Northern_Luminant.md), whose intelligence surpassed humanity's own. According to legend, they tended to avoid humanity, seeing them as a potential threat. However, after human scholars devised a method to communicate with them, and the Edenkite, partially enthralled by humanity's achievements during [The Canticlysm](The_Canticlysm.md), decided to take the risk and offered the visitors wisdom and advice. This advice was said to be cryptic and hard to decipher, leading scholars to ruminate on them for hours upon hours.[\[1\]](#cite-note-folk-1)

In the twilight years of the Canticlysm, the Tides began to rise, and the Edenkite numbered only nine. According to the myths, an unnamed steward traveled to the Northern Luminant to ask the Edenkite how to stop them, secretly wondering if the Edenkite themselves were responsible for the disaster. He questioned an Edenkite, which responded by telling him that humanity's destruction was inevitable, and suggested it to be a boon worth accepting rather than something to be fought. Furious, the steward decided to obtain the answer by force, beheading the Edenkite with [Ardour](Murmur%253A_Ardour.md) and sending its corpse crashing down onto a mountain, skewering it. The steward hacked its abdomen open and devoured its heart in an attempt to obtain the wisdom of the Edenkite, but his mind was instead overwhelmed by the information within. Filled with the memories of countless tragedies across history, he was mutated into the first [Dread Serpent](Dread_Serpent.md), a pale imitation of an Edenkite. The veracity of these claims are not clear, but it is said that the Edenkite's headless corpse is real and can be found on the mountains to this day.[\[1\]](#cite-note-folk-1)

Whether the Edenkite exist, have gone into hiding, or were driven to extinction is a matter of debate amongst modern scholars.[\[1\]](#cite-note-folk-1) Allegedly, the [Edenbrand Hellcoil](Edenbrand_Hellcoil.md) contains the remnants of an Edenkite that was slain by a hunting party that eventually found themselves lost to the [Voidsea](Voidsea.md).[\[4\]](#cite-note-edenbranddesc-4) Like all things relating to the Edenkite, whether this actually happened is up for some debate given the failure of the party to return home and their reputation lending itself to being mythologized, though the fact the Hellcoil can inflict soulfire upon its target suggests the claim may actually hold water.

The appearance of the Edenkite has yet to be properly detailed, though it is likely similar to the Dread Serpent. Edenkite are consistently described as "wyrms",[\[1\]](#cite-note-folk-1)[\[2\]](#cite-note-glossnorth-2) suggesting they either have wings but no limbs, or lack wings altogether and achieve flight through some supernatural mechanism. The [Skyreap Blade](Skyreap_Blade.md), said to be made from the bones of an Edenkite,[\[3\]](#cite-note-skyreap-3) is covered in feathers. Coupled with early accounts mistaking them for birds,[\[1\]](#cite-note-folk-1) the Edenkite may have an avian appearance of sorts. Presuming the [Edenbrand Hellcoil](Edenbrand_Hellcoil.md) to be an accurate depiction of an Edenkite's head, Edenkite posses two horns, a "beard" and two long whiskers.
