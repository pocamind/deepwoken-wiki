# Shadowcast

## Description

Shadowcast is an [Attunement](Attunements.md) that allows the player to alter their own casting of shadows, giving them abilities that focus on flame stacks and [Ether](Combat_Mechanics.md) vampirism, which is unique to Shadowcast.

Players cannot spawn with Shadowcast when first creating their character, and must be obtained by the spelltrainer in [The Depths](The_Depths.md).

## Training

Shadowcast can be trained by using a combination of the Pure Heart and Shadowcast Mantras.

Like all other elements, users can see their Mantras become stronger with practice:

-   When obtained or at Power 2, players receive the _Shadowcaster_ Talent.
-   At Shadowcast level 20, players receive the _Adept Shadowcaster_ Talent.
-   At Shadowcast level 30, players receive the _Expert Shadowcaster_ Talent.
-   Finally, at Shadowcast level 50, players receive the _Master Shadowcaster_ Talent.

## Obtaining

To unlock Shadowcast, the player must first obtain five Umbral Obsidian. Receiving this in the Etrean Luminant or [The Depths](The_Depths.md) does not matter. After obtaining them, you must deliver them to the spelltrainer in the [City of the Drowned](https://deepwoken.fandom.com/wiki/Locations#The_Depths). Like all other spelltrainers, he will give you a potion named _Nightblood_. Drinking this grants the player the Shadowcast [Attunement](Attunements.md).

### Trivia

-   In past updates the selection icon for Shadowcast had the same art as Flamecharm, but was oddly tinted white.
-   Shadowcast has a talent which blinds the victim on consecutive flame stacks, however this blind turns the victims screen white ironically.
-   Additionally, several Shadowcast moves charge up with a white light.
-   The Shadowcast trainer is dead and is insane due to their time in the depths. It can be inferred as Lightkeepers are an "authority" within Deepwoken's world that this death was not accidental.
-   Shadowcast has many moves that seem to be direct references to abilities in Rogue Lineage and Tales from the Valley, and the element seems to have much more general utility than the others.
    -   A interesting example of this would be using a blast spark on Shadow Gun, giving it a striking resemblance to Duelist Dash (and by extension, Cero Metralleta from Bleach)
